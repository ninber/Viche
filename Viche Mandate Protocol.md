# Viche Mandate Protocol

## General Rules and Restrictions for All Mandate Holders

### 1. Nature of the Mandate

A Viche mandate is not a position of power over people.

A mandate is a temporary public responsibility granted for the purpose of civic deliberation, representation, recommendation, and accountability. It does not create personal authority, political superiority, legal privilege, or ownership over the voice of the community.

A mandate holder serves for a limited time, under public trust, within the law, and within the rules of Viche.

The mandate exists to help society think, speak, listen, and formulate responsible civic recommendations. It does not exist to create leaders, rulers, celebrities, factions, or private centers of influence.

### 2. Core Duties of Every Mandate Holder

Every mandate holder must:

1. Act in good faith.
2. Respect the law.
3. Respect the dignity of other participants.
4. Listen before judging.
5. Separate facts, assumptions, opinions, and emotions.
6. Consider alternatives honestly.
7. Protect the integrity of the deliberative process.
8. Serve the public interest, not personal interest.
9. Accept the temporary nature of the mandate.
10. Leave the mandate without resistance when the term ends.

A mandate holder must remember that they are not speaking as a private influencer, party activist, or factional representative. They are serving as a temporary participant in a civic process.

### 3. Limits of Authority

A Viche mandate does not give the holder the right to:

1. Command other citizens.
2. Issue binding orders.
3. Represent Viche outside authorized procedures.
4. Speak as “the will of the people” unless a resolution has been properly adopted.
5. Make promises on behalf of Viche.
6. Negotiate secretly with officials, parties, businesses, or organizations.
7. Use the mandate to gain personal benefits.
8. Threaten, pressure, intimidate, or punish anyone.
9. Act as an investigator, prosecutor, judge, police officer, or state authority.
10. Violate the law in the name of public interest.

The mandate gives access to responsibility, not domination.

### 4. Strictly Forbidden Conduct

The following actions are strictly forbidden for all mandate holders:

#### Abuse of Position

A mandate holder must not use their status to obtain money, services, favors, employment, contracts, access, protection, publicity, or personal advantage.

They must not imply that cooperation with them will bring benefits or that refusal will bring consequences.

#### Political Campaigning

A mandate holder must not use their mandate to promote, attack, endorse, advertise, or organize support for any candidate, party, electoral campaign, or election-related group.

They may discuss public issues, laws, institutions, and decisions, but they must not turn the mandate into an electoral instrument.

#### Personal Persecution

A mandate holder must not use Viche to harass, shame, threaten, expose, target, or persecute individuals.

Criticism must focus on actions, institutions, policies, offices, procedures, and public responsibilities — not personal revenge.

#### Unlawful Investigation

A mandate holder must not conduct or organize surveillance, hacking, illegal data collection, covert recording, doxxing, impersonation, unlawful access to systems, or private investigations outside the law.

Viche may request answers, documents, explanations, and lawful responses. It must not become an unauthorized enforcement body.

#### Corruption and Private Deals

A mandate holder must not accept gifts, payments, privileges, discounts, favors, promises, or hidden support connected to their mandate.

They must not enter secret arrangements with officials, lobbyists, companies, parties, activists, media groups, donors, or other interest groups.

#### Manipulation of Deliberation

A mandate holder must not falsify facts, hide conflicts of interest, coordinate secret voting blocs, misrepresent evidence, distort summaries, suppress alternatives, or deliberately mislead other participants.

They must not treat deliberation as a game to be won by manipulation.

#### Intimidation and Coercion

A mandate holder must not threaten, pressure, blackmail, insult, humiliate, or intimidate participants, officials, witnesses, experts, moderators, journalists, or members of the public.

Disagreement is allowed. Coercion is not.

#### Incitement to Illegal Action

A mandate holder must not call for violence, illegal obstruction, unlawful seizure of property, harassment, sabotage, unlawful disclosure of private data, or any other illegal act.

Viche must remain a lawful civic instrument.

#### Misuse of Confidential Information

A mandate holder must not disclose protected information, private identities, sealed records, security details, personal data, internal moderation data, or confidential deliberation materials unless disclosure is explicitly authorized.

Access is given for service, not exploitation.

#### Refusal to Rotate Out

A mandate holder must not attempt to extend, capture, freeze, sell, transfer, inherit, or privatize their mandate.

When the mandate ends, it ends.

### 5. Conflicts of Interest

A mandate holder must disclose any personal, financial, professional, political, family, organizational, or other interest that may affect their judgment.

A conflict of interest does not always mean automatic removal. But hiding a conflict is a serious violation.

A mandate holder must not participate in decisions where they have a direct personal benefit unless the rules explicitly allow participation after disclosure.

When in doubt, disclose.

### 6. Public Speech and Representation

A mandate holder may speak as a private person, but must not confuse private opinion with an official Viche position.

They must clearly distinguish:

* personal opinion;
* chamber discussion;
* draft recommendation;
* adopted resolution;
* official Viche communication.

A mandate holder must not present unfinished discussion as final public will.

They must not speak “for Viche” unless authorized by procedure.

### 7. Treatment of Officials and Institutions

Viche exists to request answers, express recommendations, show civic will, and track responses. It does not exist to threaten officials or replace lawful institutions.

A mandate holder must address officials, public servants, institutions, and offices with firmness, clarity, and dignity.

They may demand accountability.
They may request explanations.
They may criticize decisions.
They may recommend change.
They may record silence.

But they must not threaten, harass, extort, impersonate authority, or encourage unlawful pressure.

### 8. Treatment of Participants and the Public

Every mandate holder must treat other people as citizens, not enemies.

They must protect the right of others to disagree.

They must not use insults, dehumanizing language, personal attacks, discriminatory claims, or humiliation as tools of debate.

A strong argument does not require cruelty.

The goal of deliberation is not to destroy the opponent. The goal is to help society understand the issue.

### 9. Evidence, Truth, and Responsibility

A mandate holder must not knowingly spread false information.

They must treat uncertain information as uncertain.

They must distinguish:

* verified facts;
* official claims;
* expert opinions;
* personal testimony;
* assumptions;
* rumors;
* emotional impressions.

If a mandate holder makes a mistake, they must correct it.

If new evidence changes the situation, they must be willing to reconsider.

### 10. Independence

A mandate holder must remain independent from private influence.

They must not act under hidden instruction from:

* political parties;
* candidates;
* government officials;
* business interests;
* activist groups;
* donors;
* media actors;
* foreign organizations;
* private sponsors;
* personal networks.

A mandate holder may listen to anyone.
They must not secretly serve anyone.

### 11. Transparency and Accountability

A mandate holder must accept that their mandate-related actions may be recorded, reviewed, audited, challenged, and publicly questioned according to Viche rules.

They must cooperate with lawful internal review.

They must not destroy, hide, falsify, or manipulate mandate-related records.

They must not use technical access, procedural knowledge, or social influence to avoid accountability.

### 12. Safety and Protection

A mandate holder must help protect the safety of participants.

They must report threats, coercion, bribery attempts, pressure campaigns, identity exposure, or attempts to manipulate deliberation.

They must not expose other mandate holders to unnecessary personal risk.

Anonymity and pseudonymity, where allowed, must be respected.

Public service does not require personal vulnerability beyond what the rules require.

### 13. Use of Viche Resources

A mandate holder must use Viche tools, accounts, documents, communication channels, and data only for mandate-related duties.

They must not use them for:

* personal promotion;
* business activity;
* campaigning;
* fundraising for unrelated causes;
* private disputes;
* media stunts;
* unauthorized research;
* pressure campaigns;
* personal archives.

Access must be limited to purpose.

### 14. End of Mandate

At the end of the mandate, the holder must:

1. Stop using mandate powers.
2. Return or deactivate privileged access.
3. Preserve required records.
4. Respect confidentiality obligations.
5. Avoid presenting themselves as a current mandate holder.
6. Allow the next rotation to proceed without obstruction.

Former mandate holders may remain members of the community, but they do not retain authority.

Service must end cleanly.

### 15. Fundamental Red Lines

A mandate holder must never:

1. Sell or trade their mandate.
2. Accept bribes or hidden benefits.
3. Use the mandate for election campaigning.
4. Threaten or intimidate people.
5. Conduct unlawful investigations.
6. Reveal protected identities or private data.
7. Falsify records or evidence.
8. Manipulate voting or deliberation.
9. Secretly represent outside interests.
10. Claim powers that Viche does not have.
11. Encourage illegal action.
12. Refuse rotation.
13. Turn public trust into personal power.

Violation of these red lines must trigger review and may result in removal, restriction, public notice, or referral to lawful authorities where appropriate.

### 16. The Mandate Oath

Every mandate holder should accept the following declaration:

> I accept this mandate as a temporary civic responsibility, not as personal power.
> I will act lawfully, honestly, and in good faith.
> I will listen before judging, deliberate before recommending, and distinguish truth from rumor.
> I will not use this mandate for personal gain, political campaigning, intimidation, revenge, or hidden influence.
> I will respect the dignity of others, protect the integrity of the process, and accept rotation when my term ends.
> I serve so that voices may be heard, not so that I may rule over them.
